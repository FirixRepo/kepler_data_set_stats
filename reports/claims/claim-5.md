The goal of this analysis is to estimate the average size of habitable planets and quantify the uncertainty around this estimate.

For this claim, we will work with the variable koi_prad which is the size of a planet in Earth Radii(1Radii=Size of earth)

As i was testing, the habitable planet mean size is 1.14 Earth Radii

For this claim i used a bootstrap test on a number of 1000 bootstrap permutation

With a final result of a Confidence interval at 95% of 

[0.9222884615384617 , 1.3269230769230769 ]

This suggests that the true average size of habitable planets is likely close to that of Earth, although some variability exists since this was made on 13 habitable planets.
