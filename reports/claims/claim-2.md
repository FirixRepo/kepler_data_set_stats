I want to understand if habitable planets are smaller than none habitable planets


In this claim i decided to make two groups for comparison : 

Group A : Size of habitable planets
Group B : Size of non habitable planets

My Hypothesis : 
H0 : There is no difference in size between habitable and none habitable planets

H1 : The mean size of habitable planets is inferior to the mean size of non habitable planets

Intuitively i believed that habitable planets are smaller since bigger planets tend to be mini neptunes or giant gas. And as we know they are not habitable

I use a permutation test to compare the difference in means.

The observed difference in mean is -1.461 and the p-value is 0.0286.
So it means that there is a statistically significant difference in the mean size of habitable
and non habitable planets, supporting the initial claim of habitable planets being smaller on average.
But since our data we have only 13 habitable planets against 3500 non habitable planets, 
we should be cautious about the conclusion as the sample size for habitable planets is quite small, which may affect the reliability of the results.
