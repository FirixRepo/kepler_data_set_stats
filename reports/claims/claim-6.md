I want to understand whether the difference in planet size between habitable and non-habitable planets is meaningful or just statistically significant.

For this analysis, I used the variable koi_prad, which represents the radius of a planet in Earth radii.

I separated the dataset into two groups: habitable and non-habitable planets, and computed Cohen’s d to measure the effect size of the difference in their mean sizes.

The result is:
Cohen’s d = -0.62

This corresponds to a moderate effect size.

Even though the result is statistically significant, the effect size indicates that the difference is not only statistically detectable but also meaningful in practice.

The negative value suggests that habitable planets tend to be smaller on average than non-habitable planets.
