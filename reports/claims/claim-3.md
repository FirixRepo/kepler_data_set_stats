I want to study whether or not none habitable planets from our dataset are orbiting a different star than habitable ones, as in different caraterisitics like temperature or size.

For this claim, we will have two groups on the temperature variable : 

Group A : Host star temperature of habitable planets
Group B: Host star temperature of non habitable planets
My Hypothesis : 
H0 : All planets orbit the same type of star
H1: Habitable Planets orbit different type of star

Intuitively i thought that habitable planets must orbit colder stars since temperature is also a key to judging if a planet can be habitable or not

For this claim, i used a permutation test to compare the difference in means of temperature

The observed difference in the temperature's mean is -1585.64 Kelvin resulting in a p-value really close to 0.

And since the p-value is close to 0 it means that the observed difference in means is unlikely to have occurred under the null hypothesis(all planets have the same host start carateristics), suggesting a significant  difference between the two groups.
